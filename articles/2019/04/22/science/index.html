Science

Publications

My scientific interests are in computational statistical mechanics, which is a branch of physics that deals with systems of many interacting bodies.

During my Ph.D. I have been researching the slow dynamics of dense liquids of hard spheres, and biology inspired active matter.

Here you can find my publications

  • Campo, M., Schnyder, S. K., Molina, J. J., Speck, T., & Yamamoto, R. (2019). Spontaneous Spatiotemporal Ordering of Shape Oscillations Enhances Cell Migration. arXiv preprint arXiv:1901.06707.
  • Pinchaipat, R., Campo, M., Turci, F., Hallett, J. E., Speck, T., & Royall, C. P. (2017). Experimental evidence for a structural-dynamical transition in trajectory space. Physical review letters, 119(2), 028004.

  • Campo, M. , & Speck, T. (2016). Polydisperse hard spheres: crystallization kinetics in small systems and role of local structure. Journal of Statistical Mechanics: Theory and Experiment, 2016(8), 084007.
